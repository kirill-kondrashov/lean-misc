# Plan: odd half-cube Alexander-dual reformulation

Date: 2026-03-14

## Goal

Repackage the live odd half-cube boundary frontier in simplicial-complex / Alexander-dual
language precise enough to formalize in Lean, then test whether that reformulation yields a real
structural lemma rather than a cosmetic restatement.

This is a reformulation plan, not a commitment to a proof through actual homotopy groups of
spheres.

## Why this may help

- a down-set family `𝒟 ⊆ 2^[n]` is exactly an abstract simplicial complex on `n` vertices
- `minimalOutside 𝒟` is the set of minimal nonfaces
- `upperClosure_minimalOutside_eq_compl` says the complement is generated by those minimal nonfaces
- `oddLowerHalfFamily m` is the `(m - 1)`-skeleton of the simplex on `2m + 1` vertices
- `oddMiddleLayer m` is the family of missing `m`-faces just above that skeleton
- the witness complex `oddLowerHalfFamily m` is classically a wedge-of-spheres object, so there is
  a genuine topology-facing shadow here

The realistic topology output is a statement about simplicial-complex structure, Alexander duality,
facets, links, shellability, or homology. It is not currently a route to explicit unstable
homotopy groups of spheres.

## Exact dictionary

Fix a finite vertex set `V`.

Given a down-set `𝒟 : Finset (Finset V)`, define the associated simplicial complex

`K_𝒟 := 𝒟`.

Define the finite Alexander dual candidate

`K_𝒟^vee := {s ⊆ V : V \ s ∉ 𝒟}`.

Expected exact Lean facts:

1. `K_𝒟^vee` is again a down-set.
2. `#K_𝒟^vee = 2^|V| - #𝒟`.
3. Therefore `#𝒟 = 2^(|V| - 1)` iff `#K_𝒟^vee = 2^(|V| - 1)`.
4. In odd balanced dimension, `oddLowerHalfFamily m` is fixed by duality.
5. `minimalOutside 𝒟` corresponds under complement to the facets of `K_𝒟^vee`.
6. `positiveBoundary 𝒟` corresponds under complement to faces of `K_𝒟^vee` that admit at least
   one missing one-vertex coextension.

Important caution:

- the `minimalOutside` correspondence is exact and clean
- the `positiveBoundary` correspondence is larger and less canonical
- so the dual language is most natural for the minimizer-only `minimalOutside` route, not for the
  raw boundary-count surface by itself

## Reformulated theorem surfaces

### Surface A: direct odd theorem

Current live theorem surface:

- `OddHalfCubeUpperShadowGapLowerStatement`

Topological reading:

- among simplicial complexes `K` on `2m + 1` vertices with exactly half of all subsets as faces,
  the external codimension-1 nonface boundary has size at least `choose (2m + 1) m`

This is honest as a dictionary item, but it is not yet the cleanest dual surface.

### Surface B: minimizer-only facet route

Current minimizer-side theorem surfaces:

- `OddHalfCubeBoundaryGlobalMinimizerMinimalOutsideLowerStatement`
- `OddHalfCubeBoundaryGlobalMinimizerPositiveBoundaryAntichainStatement`

These translate more cleanly.

Under complement / Alexander dual:

- `minimalOutside 𝒟` becomes the facet set of `K_𝒟^vee`
- therefore the minimizer-only `minimalOutside` lower bound becomes:
  every dual of an odd half-cube global boundary minimizer has at least
  `choose (2m + 1) m` facets

If, in addition, the minimizer-side antichain statement forces

- `positiveBoundary 𝒟 = minimalOutside 𝒟`

then the direct boundary count becomes exactly a facet-count statement on the dual side.

### Surface C: archival false universal route

The false theorem

- `OddHalfCubeMinimalOutsideLowerStatement`

would have translated to:

- every half-cube simplicial complex on `2m + 1` vertices has dual with at least
  `choose (2m + 1) m` facets

The dimension-3 counterexample shows this universal facet-lower-bound statement is false.
So the topology route must stay minimizer-only or otherwise add a stronger true hypothesis.

## Extremizer picture under duality

For the witness family:

- `oddLowerHalfFamily m = {s : |s| <= m}`
- `oddMiddleLayer m = {s : |s| = m + 1}`

Consequences to formalize:

1. `oddLowerHalfFamily m` is Alexander self-dual in dimension `2m + 1`.
2. The facets of `oddLowerHalfFamily m` are exactly the `m`-subsets.
3. The minimal nonfaces are exactly the `(m + 1)`-subsets.
4. Hence the witness carries both counts:
   - number of facets: `choose (2m + 1) m`
   - number of minimal nonfaces: `choose (2m + 1) m`

This is the cleanest actual sphere-adjacent picture:

- the witness is a simplex skeleton
- simplex skeleta have classical wedge-of-spheres homotopy type
- the middle binomial coefficient is showing up as a facet/minimal-nonface count, not yet as a
  homotopy-group computation

## Most promising topology-facing subquestions

1. Does global boundary minimality force the dual complex to be pure?
2. Does lower-boundary-slice vanishing translate to a no-low-rank-exposed-face statement on the
   dual side?
3. Can the minimizer-side antichain statement be reframed as "all exposed dual boundary faces are
   facets"?
4. Does the dual of a global minimizer satisfy shellability, shiftedness, or a Morse-theoretic
   property strong enough to lower-bound the number of facets?
5. Is there a self-duality or near-self-duality principle for actual odd minimizers, beyond the
   model witness `oddLowerHalfFamily`?

## No-go points

1. Do not claim a route through explicit unstable homotopy groups `pi_k(S^n)` without a concrete
   mechanism.
2. Do not treat a pure rephrasing of `positiveBoundary` as progress unless it yields a new true
   structural lemma.
3. Do not revive the already-false universal `minimalOutside` theorem in topological language.
4. Do not let equality-case topology replace the live lower-bound target unless the dual route
   actually forces a usable minimizer theorem.
