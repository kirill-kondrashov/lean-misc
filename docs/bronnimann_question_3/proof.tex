\begin{abstract}
  This document records the current Lean formalization around Br\"onnimann's Question 3: whether
  there exists a group with solvable word problem and irrational geodesic growth. The repository
  contains a fully internal theorem reducing the question to the existence of a concrete witness
  with the required properties. The repository also contains a formal presentation of Bodart's
  virtually Engel group from \texttt{arXiv:2306.10381}, together with an explicit computable
  coordinate model into which the presentation maps. The solvable-word-problem witness for the
  virtually Engel presentation is derived internally from injectivity of the coordinate
  homomorphism; the irrational-growth witness remains to be derived from Bodart's asymptotic
  estimate.
\end{abstract}

\maketitle

\section{Introduction}

Br\"onnimann asked in 2016 whether there exists a group with solvable word problem and irrational
geodesic growth. Bodart's construction of a virtually Engel group with intermediate geodesic
growth gives such an example. The formalization considered here proves the general implication that
any presented group equipped with a finite generating alphabet, solvable word problem, and
irrational geodesic growth yields a positive answer to Br\"onnimann's question. The Bodart witness
is formalized at the level of its presentation, its geodesic alphabet, and an explicit computable
coordinate model. The final instantiation is not claimed until the witness properties are proved
internally from that structure.

\section{Context}

The formalization has three parts:
\begin{itemize}
  \item generic definitions for geodesic growth and solvable word problem;
  \item a formal presentation of Bodart's virtually Engel witness together with a computable
    coordinate model;
  \item a fully local witness-packaging theorem.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Disclaimer and Verification}
The witness-packaging theorem is checked by the Lean kernel and uses only the base axioms. The
application to Bodart's group presently depends on one literature fact, Bodart's asymptotic
theorem, and two internal-facing axioms: injectivity of the coordinate homomorphism from the
virtually Engel presentation into its coordinate model, and irrationality of the geodesic growth
(the latter still to be derived from the asymptotic theorem via the asymptotic-to-irrationality
bridge). The solvable-word-problem witness is derived internally from injectivity of the
coordinate homomorphism.

\section{Logical Dependencies}

The argument has the following form.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
  \item One defines the word problem and geodesic growth for a finitely generated group presented
    by generators and relators.
  \item One fixes a criterion for rationality of the geodesic-growth series in terms of eventual
    linear recurrences with constant coefficients.
  \item One proves that any witness with solvable word problem and irrational geodesic growth gives
    a positive answer to Br\"onnimann's Question~3.
  \item One records Bodart's virtually Engel group, constructs an explicit computable coordinate
    model for it, and verifies the defining relators in that model.
  \item It remains to derive injectivity of the coordinate homomorphism internally, from which the
    solvable-word-problem witness is already obtained by direct decidability of coordinates, and
    to derive from Bodart's asymptotic estimate the irrational-growth witness.
\end{enumerate}
