\begin{abstract}
  This document records the current Lean formalization around Br\"onnimann's Question 3: whether
  there exists a group with solvable word problem and irrational geodesic growth. The final goal
  of the development is a fully formalized unconditional positive answer to Question~3, obtained
  by instantiating the witness-packaging theorem with Bodart's virtually Engel group from
  \texttt{arXiv:2306.10381}. At present the abstract witness-packaging theorem is checked by the
  Lean kernel and uses only the base axioms. The Bodart-side application depends on three
  internal-facing axioms that pinpoint the exact algebraic and analytic gaps still to be
  discharged; a single consolidated section at the end of this document lists them.
\end{abstract}

\maketitle

\section{Introduction}

Br\"onnimann asked in 2016 whether there exists a group with solvable word problem and irrational
geodesic growth. Bodart's construction of a virtually Engel group with intermediate geodesic
growth gives such an example. The formalization considered here proves the general implication that
any presented group equipped with a finite generating alphabet, solvable word problem, and
irrational geodesic growth yields a positive answer to Br\"onnimann's question. The Bodart witness
is formalized at the level of its presentation, its geodesic alphabet, and an explicit computable
coordinate model. The final instantiation is not claimed until the witness properties are proved
internally from that structure.

\section{Context}

The formalization has three parts:
\begin{itemize}
  \item generic definitions for geodesic growth and solvable word problem;
  \item a formal presentation of Bodart's virtually Engel witness together with a computable
    coordinate model;
  \item a fully local witness-packaging theorem.
\end{itemize}

\section{Logical Dependencies}

The argument has the following form.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
  \item One defines the word problem and geodesic growth for a finitely generated group presented
    by generators and relators.
  \item One fixes a criterion for rationality of the geodesic-growth series in terms of eventual
    linear recurrences with constant coefficients.
  \item One proves that any witness with solvable word problem and irrational geodesic growth
    gives a positive answer to Br\"onnimann's Question~3.
  \item One records Bodart's virtually Engel group, constructs an explicit computable coordinate
    model for it, and verifies the defining relators in that model.
  \item One derives the solvable-word-problem witness for the virtually Engel presentation from
    injectivity of the coordinate homomorphism.
  \item The remaining internal steps needed to reach an unconditional positive answer are
    consolidated in \S\ref{sec:RemainingSteps}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Remaining Internal Steps}
\label{sec:RemainingSteps}

To reach a fully formalized unconditional positive answer to Question~3 it remains to discharge
the following three axioms currently recorded in the Bodart-side development. Each is stated as
an internal-facing axiom to be proved, not as an imported literature fact.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(A\arabic*)]
  \item \emph{Injectivity of the coordinate homomorphism from the virtually Engel presentation
    into its computable coordinate model.} The framework reducing this to a single
    multiplication-compatibility identity for the Hall normal-form reconstruction is already in
    place: the image of every element of the presented group is proved to lie in the coordinate
    lattice, injectivity is proved equivalent to a canonical set-theoretic retract being a left
    inverse, and the retract identity is checked on the trivial element and on each presentation
    generator. The remaining obligation is the multiplication-compatibility identity for the
    retract restricted to the lattice, whose input is the Hall commutation identities implied by
    the four defining relators.
  \item \emph{Bodart's asymptotic estimate for the geodesic-growth function}
    $\gamma_{\mathrm{geod}}(n)\asymp \exp(n^{3/5}\log n)$. This is the main quantitative result
    of Bodart's paper, to be formalized from the geometric path-counting construction over the
    virtually Engel group.
  \item \emph{Asymptotic-to-irrationality bridge}, i.e.\ the theorem that an integer sequence
    satisfying an eventual linear recurrence with constant coefficients cannot have intermediate
    growth of the form $\exp(n^{3/5}\log n)$. Combined with axiom~(A2), this yields the
    irrational-growth witness for the virtually Engel presentation with respect to the alphabet
    $S=\{a,a^{-1},t\}$.
\end{enumerate}
Once (A1), (A2), and (A3) are proved internally, the witness-packaging theorem instantiated at
Bodart's virtually Engel group yields the unconditional positive answer.
